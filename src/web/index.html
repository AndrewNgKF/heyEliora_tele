Rather host it yourself? Go for it.

Clone the repo, add your keys, and you have your own AI assistant.

# Clone it
$ git clone https://github.com/AndrewNgKF/heyEliora_tele.git
$ cd heyEliora_tele
 
# Set it up
$ bun install
$ cp .env.example .env
 
# Run it
$ bun run src/index.js

You'll need a Telegram Bot Token, an Anthropic API Key, and a Turso database.

Questions.

Is my data safe?

Type /mydata to see everything Eliora knows about you — goals, preferences, activity, even her internal notes. Type /forget to wipe it all. Self-host and we never see it. The code is open source — audit exactly what gets stored.

Why Telegram?

Nobody needs another tab. Telegram is fast, on every device, and push notifications just work. A personal assistant should meet you where you are.

What AI model does she use?

Claude by Anthropic. Free uses Haiku (fast). Pro uses Sonnet (most capable). Self-hosters can swap the model.

How is this different from ChatGPT?

ChatGPT forgets you after every conversation. Eliora remembers your goals, tracks your streaks, notices your avoidance patterns, and reaches out to you proactively when you go quiet. She also draws from domain experts — Attia for health, Naval for startups, Pressfield for creative work — and applies their frameworks to your specific situation. She's not an everything-machine. She's your personal agentic AI.

Will she support calendar, email, docs?

Calendar and email integrations are on the roadmap. But honestly, you already have a calendar app. What you don't have is something that connects your calendar to your goals and tells you when you're spending time on the wrong things. That's where Eliora is headed.
